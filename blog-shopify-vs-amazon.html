Shopify vs Amazon: Which Platform Is Better in 2025?

Shopify and Amazon are the two giants of ecommerce. But which one should you choose in 2025? This complete comparison breaks down costs, control, traffic, profits, and scalability to help you make the right decision for your business.

Overview: Shopify vs Amazon in 2025

Shopify is a complete ecommerce platform where you build and own your online store. Amazon is a massive marketplace where you sell alongside millions of other sellers. Both have strengths, but they serve different business goals.

💡 INBLINK Tip: Many successful brands use both platforms — Shopify for brand building and Amazon for high-volume sales. The best strategy depends on your goals, budget, and long-term vision.

Pros & Cons Comparison

Factor Shopify Amazon
Ownership ✅ Full brand ownership ❌ Limited control
Traffic Source You build it Built-in (huge)
Fees $29–$299/mo + transaction fees 15%+ referral + FBA fees
Customization High Low
Customer Data ✅ Yours ❌ Amazon's

Shopify Strengths in 2025

  • Full Brand Control: Design your store exactly how you want. Build customer loyalty and email lists.
  • Lower Long-term Fees: No heavy referral fees once you drive your own traffic.
  • Flexibility: Sell physical products, digital downloads, subscriptions, or services.
  • SEO & Marketing Freedom: Rank on Google, run Meta/TikTok ads without restrictions.
  • Multi-channel Selling: Easily connect to Amazon, eBay, Walmart, and social platforms.

Amazon Strengths in 2025

  • Instant Traffic: Access to hundreds of millions of buyers actively searching to buy.
  • FBA Logistics: Amazon handles storage, packing, shipping, and customer service.
  • Prime Advantage: Eligible products get massive visibility and trust.
  • Fast Scaling: Easier to test products and scale winners quickly.

Cost Breakdown 2025

Shopify: $29/month (Basic) + 2% transaction fees (or less with Shopify Payments) + apps + ads.

Amazon: 15% referral fee + FBA fulfillment fees + storage fees + PPC ads. Can easily reach 30–40% total fees.

Winner: Shopify has better margins once you have consistent traffic.

Who Should Choose Which?

Choose Shopify if you want to:

  • Build a long-term brand
  • Own your customer data
  • Have full design control
  • Sell unique or high-margin products

Choose Amazon if you want to:

  • Validate products quickly
  • Focus only on fulfillment (use FBA)
  • Sell commodity products
  • Get fast sales with minimal marketing

The Hybrid Approach (Recommended)

Smart brands in 2025 use both: Build your brand on Shopify and expand reach via Amazon FBA. This gives you the best of both worlds — brand ownership + massive traffic.

Conclusion

Shopify gives you freedom and higher long-term profits. Amazon gives you speed and built-in demand. Your choice depends on your goals. Most serious sellers eventually use both platforms.
