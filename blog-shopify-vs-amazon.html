Shopify vs Amazon: Which Platform Is Better in 2025?

INBLINK Team
Ecommerce Growth Experts

Shopify and Amazon are the two giants of ecommerce. But which one should you choose in 2025? This complete comparison breaks down costs, control, traffic, profits, and scalability to help you make the right decision for your business.

Overview: Shopify vs Amazon in 2025

Shopify is a complete ecommerce platform where you build and own your online store. Amazon is a massive marketplace where you sell alongside millions of other sellers. Both have strengths, but they serve different business goals.

💡 INBLINK Tip: Many successful brands use both platforms — Shopify for brand building and Amazon for high-volume sales. The best strategy depends on your goals, budget, and long-term vision.

Pros & Cons Comparison

Factor Shopify Amazon
Ownership ✅ Full brand ownership ❌ Limited control
Traffic You build it yourself Built-in (300M+ buyers)
Fees $29–$299/mo + transaction 15%+ referral + FBA fees
Customization High — full design control Low — fixed templates
Customer Data ✅ 100% yours ❌ Amazon owns it
Logistics You manage or outsource ✅ FBA handles everything

Shopify Strengths in 2025

  • Full Brand Control: Design your store exactly how you want. Build customer loyalty and email lists you own forever.
  • Lower Long-term Fees: No heavy referral fees once you drive consistent traffic — better margins at scale.
  • Flexibility: Sell physical products, digital downloads, subscriptions, or services all in one place.
  • SEO & Marketing Freedom: Rank on Google, run Meta/TikTok ads, and build retargeting audiences without restrictions.
  • Multi-channel Selling: Easily sync inventory and sell on Amazon, eBay, Walmart, and social platforms from Shopify.

Amazon Strengths in 2025

  • Instant Traffic: Access to 300M+ active buyers who are already searching to buy — no ad spend required to get started.
  • FBA Logistics: Amazon handles storage, packing, shipping, returns, and customer service for you completely.
  • Prime Advantage: FBA products get Prime badge — massive visibility boost and built-in customer trust.
  • Fast Product Validation: List a product today and get sales within days — much faster than building a Shopify store from scratch.

Cost Breakdown 2025

Shopify: $29/month (Basic plan) + 2.9% transaction fees (waived with Shopify Payments) + app costs + paid ads. Total: $100–$500/month to run properly.

Amazon: $39.99/month Professional plan + 15% referral fee on every sale + FBA fulfilment fees ($3–$5 per unit) + PPC ads. Total fees can reach 30–40% of revenue.

📌 Verdict on Costs: Shopify has significantly better margins once you have consistent traffic. Amazon fees compound quickly at scale and eat into profits heavily.

Who Should Choose Which?

Choose Shopify if you want to:

  • Build a long-term recognizable brand
  • Own your customer email list and data
  • Have full design and marketing control
  • Sell unique, high-margin, or branded products
  • Run dropshipping or subscription box models

Choose Amazon if you want to:

  • Validate product demand quickly without building an audience
  • Outsource fulfilment completely via FBA
  • Sell commodity or high-demand products
  • Get fast sales without heavy marketing investment

The Hybrid Approach (Recommended for 2025)

Smart brands in 2025 don't choose one — they use both strategically. Build your brand on Shopify for long-term growth, customer ownership, and higher margins. Use Amazon FBA to tap into instant demand, validate new products, and reach buyers who'd never find you otherwise.

Tools like Linnworks, Sellbrite, or Shopify's Amazon channel make managing both from one dashboard straightforward once you're at scale.

Conclusion

Shopify gives you freedom, brand ownership, and higher long-term profits. Amazon gives you speed, built-in demand, and logistics simplicity. Your choice depends entirely on your business goals and current stage.

If you're starting out and want fast sales — Amazon. If you want to build something lasting that you own — Shopify. If you're serious about ecommerce — eventually both.
